Topic number selection

To determine the optimal number of topics, I implemented a bootstrap validation approach using 10 iterations for each configuration, testing a range from 10 to 150 topics. This method involves randomly resampling the corpus to train the LDA model on 10 different versions of the data for every potential topic count, allowing for the calculation of a robust and stable mean semantic coherence. Rather than relying on a single training run, this statistical repetition identifies the configuration that maximizes result quality while minimizing sensitivity to randomness, ensuring a final model that is both reliable and representative of the text's actual structure.

The optimal model complexity was identified at 70 topics.

LLM Analysis

Questions for LLM

To what extent do the analyzed texts give a central place to literature and how is the figure of the author mobilized? Is it to engage with literary themes or to serve as a cultural, historical, or political symbol?

This question challenges the idea that mentioning a target Russian Author necessarily implies a focus on them as a literary figure.
  • Centrality of literature: Scored (0-2).
  • Context of citation: Alone, absent, or with others.
  • Entity Identification: Identify every name and precise if the person is an author, how many time he's cited and the nationality.

Results & Evaluation

After manually annotating the sampled articles, the initial results showed low accuracy across different models and prompts.
